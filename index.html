Home · The Sanctuary
Welcome, Traveller

You have found your way to this quiet corner of the Wired — a sanctuary built by hand, in notepad, during the long hours when the rest of the world was sleeping. I am glad you are here.

This place is devoted to the things that have lived in my heart longest: the cursed island of Lodoss, the digital ghost of Lain, old animation that moves like it was made by people who believed in what they were drawing. If any of that means something to you, stay a while.

— ⚔ ✦ ⚔ —

I coded this myself. It has mistakes in it. The code is a mess. That is fine — all living things are a little broken, and this website is alive in its own small way.

The Wanderer

I go by Ashara here. I have been on the internet since I was very small, which means I remember when websites were places people actually lived in. This is my attempt to have one of those again.

I am drawn to things that are old, or that feel old: hand-drawn animation, fantasy novels with maps inside the cover, the particular quiet of a computer at three in the morning. Record of Lodoss War found me at the right age and never really left.

Shrine · Pirotess of Marmo

Dark elf. Shadow of Ashram. The most faithful figure in a story full of faithless people. This shrine exists because Pirotess deserves one — because loyalty written this quietly is rare in any medium.

— ⚔ —

She appears without announcement and leaves the same way. The animation gives her just enough frames to be real. Her eyes are the colour of deep water. When Ashram falls, she is there. When he rises, she is also there. The series does not explain this. It does not need to.

What I love most: she is never rescued. She is never waiting. She simply walks alongside, as an equal who has chosen a direction and follows it to its end. That is a complete character. The OVA understood something.
